# v2.1.0 density-cluster pilot and human-review gate

**Status:** complete. Three live ORS routes were generated, reviewed by Paulo on
2026-07-18, compiled, and independently validated in both public city packs.

## Contract

- The visitor map loads a deterministic 3,500-record highlight pack first.
- The complete 185,307-record inventory loads only for search, filters, or the
  explicit map switch.
- Trail areas qualify by overall public-tree density before a theme is applied.
- A stop is a 60–75 m tree-rich area with at least eight distinct City records
  and at least three theme matches.
- Each trail has three to five non-overlapping areas and is walking only.
- OpenRouteService `foot-walking` supplies snapping, a distance matrix, routed
  geometry, actual distance/duration, and engine/graph provenance.
- A route may be a loop when its routed overhead is reasonable; otherwise it is
  point-to-point.
- Generated and routed candidates remain `NOT HUMAN REVIEWED` and cannot enter
  the runtime artifact.

## Three pilots

The deterministic packet contains Mount Pleasant / Cherry blossoms,
Grandview-Woodland / Fruit-tree families, and Kitsilano / Maples. Friendly common
names lead visitor-facing text; scientific identity remains available in tree
details.

The pinned routed packet records a 6.1 km Mount Pleasant loop, 3.6 km
Grandview-Woodland point-to-point walk, and 3.3 km Kitsilano point-to-point walk.
All anchor snaps are within 40 m, every leg is within 2 km, and repeated route
geometry remains below 5% for each pilot.

## Quality gates

The compiler rejects missing trees, repeated cluster membership, malformed
anchors, route-order drift, routes outside city bounds, non-ORS/non-walking
provenance, snap distances over 40 m, legs over 2 km, excessive repeated
geometry, unsupported claims, missing human identity/date, and source provenance
drift.

The standalone review artifact displays clusters, individual records, theme
matches, path/order, actual distance, route provenance, editable name/narrative,
required unknown limitations, and approve/reject decisions. It disables approval
until the pilot is routed.

## Publication result

`trails-review.json` contains three compiler-valid human-reviewed records. The
full pack and startup pack contain the same three trails and exact reverse
membership for 1,034 distinct City tree records. The startup pack grew from
3,500 to 4,534 records so every trail member is available when a visitor opens
the catalogue. `city:validate-published` proves the approved source, compiled
full pack, and deterministic highlight pack agree exactly.
